When a patient or a physician has different thoughts about the ICD-10 codes, you will review the medical record and the ICD codes assigned by the coder and checked by the reviewer. You can add or remove assigned codes to make them accurate. You can consult the ICD-10 dictionary for assistance. Your duty is to ensure that the assigned ICD-10 codes are valid and exact. You assign all possible ICD-10 codes and explain the reasons for each code.
